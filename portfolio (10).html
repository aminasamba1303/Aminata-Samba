Creative
Direction

Shaping the full vision from first concept to final image. Every element answers a single question: what world are we building?

Creative direction is the architecture of a visual world. I enter a project at its most formative stage when an idea is still a feeling and I build the structure that turns that feeling into a cohesive, intentional image.

My approach is non-linear. I work simultaneously across concept, mood, casting direction, styling, set, and atmosphere. Everything must breathe together before a single frame is shot.

The result is never just a photograph. It's a world with its own logic, its own emotional temperature, its own reason for existing.

My approach

  • Concept development from cultural or emotional reference
  • Mood architecture building the feeling before the image
  • Cross-discipline direction: styling, makeup, set, light
  • Narrative framing every image must carry a story
  • Final image curation and sequencing

002

Art
Direction

Visual language as identity. The frame, the palette, the texture nothing is accidental, everything is decided.

Art direction is the act of making taste visible. It's deciding that this image should feel like a humid night in Dakar rather than a crisp Paris afternoon and then making every single element serve that feeling.

I work with color, texture, proportion, and contrast not as technical variables but as emotional instruments. The palette of a shoot is its first language. The framing is its posture.

My art direction is always rooted in cultural reference I build visual systems that carry history while feeling entirely of the present.

My approach

  • Color palette development as emotional direction
  • Cultural and archival visual research
  • Typography and graphic identity for editorial work
  • Lighting direction mood through light temperature
  • Post-treatment direction for visual coherence

003

Mood-
boarding

A moodboard is not a collage. It is a world compressed into a single emotional truth the first proof that the vision is real.

A moodboard is the moment a feeling becomes a direction. I build moodboards that don't just show references they make an argument. Every image chosen is a commitment to a specific emotional register.

My moodboards work across multiple layers simultaneously: the visual atmosphere, the cultural references, the colour logic, the styling direction, the pose language all speaking the same language before a word has been said on set.

A strong moodboard eliminates ambiguity. It aligns every collaborator around a shared vision before production begins.

My approach

  • Emotional architecture mood before image
  • Cultural layering: archival, contemporary, personal
  • Multi-layer boards: vibe, styling, makeup, set, poses
  • Presentation as narrative boards that tell a story

Editorial
Shoots

On set, every decision is the story. Light, posture, silence between frames I direct what the camera sees and what it feels.

An editorial shoot is where the world I've been building finally becomes physical. My role on set is to hold the vision to make sure every decision made in real time serves the emotional intention established in pre-production.

I direct talent with the sensibility of someone who has been in front of the camera. I understand presence. I understand what it means to be looked at and how to make that look intentional.

My background as an actress means I give direction that is embodied, not technical. I describe feelings, not positions.

My approach

  • Talent direction rooted in emotional language
  • Real-time mood management on set
  • Collaboration with photographer for visual coherence
  • Styling and makeup adjustments during shoot
  • Image selection and narrative sequencing

Styling &
Composition

Garments, textures, and space arranged with the instinct of someone who has inhabited the image not just observed it.

Styling, for me, is never about the garment alone. It is about what the garment says when it meets the body, the light, and the mood of the world being built. Every piece is chosen as part of an argument.

I work with proportion, texture, and cultural resonance simultaneously combining archival references with contemporary pieces to create looks that feel both specific and timeless.

Composition extends beyond the frame: it is the relationship between the body, the set, the negative space, and the eye. I direct all of these as a unified visual system.

My approach

  • Styling as cultural and emotional language
  • Archival + contemporary fashion combination
  • Accessory and adornment as narrative detail
  • Set composition body in space
  • Textural and chromatic coherence across looks

Brand
Storytelling

Luxury, fashion, culture told through imagery that doesn't explain itself. The brand becomes a world. The world becomes a feeling.

I approach brand storytelling as world-building. A brand is not a logo or a colour palette it is a system of values made visible. My role is to translate those values into images that feel inevitable rather than constructed.

This means building visual environments where the product, the talent, the styling, and the atmosphere all reinforce a single clear emotional proposition: this is who we are, and this is why it matters.

I work particularly with brands in the fashion, luxury, and cultural space identities that need their imagery to carry weight, history, and contemporary relevance simultaneously.

My approach

  • Brand identity translated into visual language
  • Cultural positioning through editorial imagery
  • Campaign concept development
  • Visual identity consistency across deliverables
  • Fashion and luxury brand aesthetic direction

The Concept

A love letter to West African femininity

Jongoma is a love letter written in fabric, gold and posture. The starting point: the women who raised me. My mother, my aunts, the grandes dames of Senegalese femininity. Women who dressed with an authority that needed no explanation. Boubous in choup and bassin, gold stacked on gold, silhouettes that filled a room before a word was spoken. A maximalism that was never performance. It was identity.

The project takes that visual language and translates it into something of now. The traditional poses, the dignity, the stillness, the weight of a woman who knows she is being looked at, are preserved. What shifts is the context and the detail. Sneakers where leather slippers might have been. A gallery or museum as the backdrop, so the body becomes one object among many, a living work in a space built to hold art.

That tension is the concept: the archive and the present, the inherited and the chosen, tradition as a starting point rather than a destination.

Creative Process

Direction notes

References pulled from family archives, Malick Sidibé, studio portraiture from Dakar and Saint-Louis. The palette drawn from earth, amber and indigo. The styling built around volume, jewellery and posture.

The Studio

KOZZY Studio

KOZZY Studio is a Montréal creative space built around objects with a point of view. Vintage televisions, curated props, the kind of backdrop that demands intention. The owner reached out to bring a fashion and design sensibility to what the studio holds: not a catalogue of available props, but a visual world waiting to be inhabited. The brief was to elevate. To show that these objects don't just sit in a frame. They shape it.

The Concept

Maximalism on mute.

The TV set is off. The styling is on. The concept plays on that tension: screens that no longer broadcast anything, placed against a body that says everything. Maximalist without being loud, edgy without being cold. The fur, the tie, the patent boots, the rings: each piece chosen to clash with the analogue stillness of the televisions and win.

Shot in both colour and black and white to honour the dual nature of the objects. Their retro weight and their contemporary reframe.

The uninvited exhibit

The Brand

Artelier Saint

Artelier Saint curates design objects with a collector's instinct and a stylist's eye. Vintage finds, intentional spaces, colour that earns its place. The collaboration came through Qauffeegraphy. What I brought to it was art direction and fashion sensibility: a gaze more concerned with the body, the styling, the frame.

The Concept

The Body as Object, The Object as Body

The brief was to elevate, not to demonstrate. Not a catalogue shot, not a lifestyle image. Instead: a dialogue between a body and an object, each shaping the meaning of the other. The LC4 Chaise Longue was never just a chair. It was designed to support the human body in its most natural, most free position. The editorial takes that premise and makes it visible through posture, through tension, through the way a woman inhabits a piece of design history.

The grain. The black and white. The studio silence. Everything in the frame serves the object and its inhabitant equally.

About me

Aminata
Samba

Creative Director — Art Director
Montréal · Paris · Dakar

Based in Montréal, shaped by Paris and Dakar. Aminata Samba is a Creative Director and Art Director working at the intersection of fashion, editorial and cultural identity — building visual worlds for projects that need imagery with weight and presence.

Her eye moves between registers: a crystal crown in the Pinault Collection, vintage televisions repurposed as fashion sets, a tribute to Donyale Luna's erased legacy, a West African identity reclaimed through high fashion. Alongside the creative practice — a background in communication sciences and marketing strategy that brings brand thinking to every visual decision. The common thread is an instinct for what a body communicates before a word is spoken — femininity not in exposure, but in charisma.

I don't document. I direct. Every image I make is felt before it is understood.
